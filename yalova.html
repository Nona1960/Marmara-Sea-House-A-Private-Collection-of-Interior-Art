Yalova

Yalova is one of Turkey’s oldest seaside resorts, situated on the eastern shore of the Marmara Sea. For centuries it has been known for its mild climate, fertile gardens and renowned thermal springs. In the early twentieth century, the city became a favourite retreat of Mustafa Kemal Atatürk, who spent time here for rest and work, leaving Yalova with a lasting place in the cultural history of modern Turkey.

Today, the city is celebrated for its elegant waterfront promenade lined with monumental blocks of white Marmara marble, beautifully maintained flower gardens and green parks stretching along the sea. Traditional bakeries, lively fish and farmers’ markets, cafés and local shops preserve the atmosphere of an authentic Turkish coastal town where everyday life continues at a relaxed Mediterranean pace.

The apartment documented in this archive is located on the first line of the seafront park, directly overlooking the pedestrian promenade. Throughout the day, local residents gather here to walk beneath the trees, enjoy the sea breeze, meet friends and watch the ships crossing the Marmara Sea. This privileged setting combines the tranquillity of a private residence with the vibrant rhythm of one of Turkey’s most charming coastal cities.
